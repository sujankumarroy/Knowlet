2. Short Answer Questions

(a) Write two federal features of the Constitution of India.

  • Written Constitution: It is the supreme law that clearly defines the powers of both Central and State governments.
  • Division of Powers: Powers are divided between the Union and the States through the three lists (Union, State, and Concurrent) in the Seventh Schedule.

(b) What is Preamble?

[span_15](start_span)

The Preamble is an introductory statement to the Constitution that sets out the guiding purpose, principles, and philosophy of the document[span_15](end_span). It represents the "spirit" of the Constitution.

3. Descriptive Questions

(a) Explain the role of the Constituent Assembly in framing of the Indian Constitution.

The Constituent Assembly, formed in 1946 under the Cabinet Mission Plan, played a monumental role in drafting the Constitution:

  • Representation: It consisted of indirectly elected members from provincial assemblies, representing various shades of Indian political thought.
  • Drafting Committee: Led by Dr. B.R. [span_21](start_span)Ambedkar, this committee synthesized various global constitutional ideas into the Indian context[span_21](end_span).
  • Deliberation: Every clause was debated extensively over nearly three years (2 years, 11 months, and 18 days) to ensure consensus.

(b) Explain the ideals enshrined in the Preamble of the Indian Constitution.

The Preamble enshrines several core ideals:

  • Sovereign: India is independent of any external power.
  • Socialist & Secular: Added by the 42nd Amendment, these emphasize economic equality and equal respect for all religions.
  • Democratic Republic: Power lies with the people, and the head of the state is elected.
  • Justice, Liberty, and Equality: It aims to secure social, economic, and political justice; liberty of thought and expression; and equality of status and opportunity for all citizens.

6. Descriptive Questions

(a) Discuss the Fundamental Duties of the Indian citizens.

Enshrined in Part IV-A (Article 51A), there are 11 Fundamental Duties:

  • To abide by the Constitution and respect the National Flag and Anthem.
  • To protect the sovereignty, unity, and integrity of India.
  • To promote harmony and the spirit of common brotherhood.
  • To protect and improve the natural environment.
  • To provide opportunities for education to children between 6-14 years (added by the 86th Amendment).

(b) Write in detail the provisions enumerated under the Right to Freedom.

The Right to Freedom (Articles 19–22) is a core cluster of liberties:

  • Article 19: Guarantees six freedoms—speech and expression, assembly, association, movement, residence, and profession.
  • Article 20: Protection against arbitrary and excessive punishment.
  • Article 21: Protection of life and personal liberty.
  • Article 22: Protection against arrest and detention in certain cases.

9. Descriptive Questions

(a) Discuss the procedure of election of the President of India.

The President is elected indirectly by an Electoral College:

  • Composition: Elected members of both Houses of Parliament and elected members of Legislative Assemblies of all States/UTs.
  • Method: System of proportional representation by means of a single transferable vote via secret ballot.
  • Value of Votes: Formulae are used to ensure parity between the States as a whole and the Union.

(b) Under what circumstances, the President of India can declare National Emergency? Discuss the effects.

Circumstances (Article 352): Declared on grounds of war, external aggression, or armed rebellion.

Effects:

  • Federal Structure: The Union government gains power to give directions to States on any matter.
    • [span_76](start_span)
  • Fundamental Rights: Rights under Article 19 are automatically suspended; others (except Arts. 20 & 21) can be suspended by Presidential order[span_76](end_span).
  • Legislature: The life of the Lok Sabha can be extended by one year at a time.

12. Descriptive Questions

(a) Discuss the composition, powers and functions of the Union Council of Ministers.

Composition: Includes the Prime Minister and three categories of ministers—Cabinet Ministers, Ministers of State, and Deputy Ministers.

Powers & Functions:

  • Executive: Administers the country through various departments and civil services.
  • Financial: Prepared the annual budget and controls national expenditure.
    • [span_96](start_span)
  • Legislative: Most bills are "Government Bills" introduced by ministers[span_96](end_span).

(b) Explain the relationship between the Prime Minister and the Union Council of Ministers.

The PM is the "primus inter pares" (first among equals):

  • Selection: The PM decides who will be included in the Council.
  • Allocation: The PM assigns portfolios and can reshuffle them at any time.
    • [span_102](start_span)
  • Coordination: The PM coordinates the work of different departments and presides over meetings[span_102](end_span).
  • Resignation: If the PM resigns, the entire Council of Ministers stands dissolved.

14. Short Answer Questions

(a) What are the different types of Bills that are introduced in the Parliament?

  • Ordinary Bills: Related to matters other than financial.
  • Money Bills: Related to taxation and expenditure.
  • Constitutional Amendment Bills: To amend the Constitution.

(b) Write a note on advisory jurisdiction of the Supreme Court of India.

Under Article 143, the President can seek the Supreme Court's opinion on questions of law or fact of public importance. The opinion given by the court is not legally binding on the President.

15. Descriptive Questions

(a) Discuss the composition, powers and functions of the Rajya Sabha.

Composition: Maximum 250 members—238 representing States/UTs and 12 nominated by the President.

Powers & Functions:

  • Legislative: Every non-money bill must be passed by Rajya Sabha.
  • Financial: Limited powers in money bills (can only suggest changes within 14 days).
  • Special Powers: Can authorize Parliament to make laws on state subjects (Art. 249) or create All India Services (Art. 312).

(b) What is judicial review? Discuss the scope in India.

Definition: The power of the judiciary to examine the constitutionality of legislative enactments and executive orders.

Scope: If any law or order violates the Constitution, the Supreme Court/High Courts can declare it null and void. In India, this power is based on the "procedure established by law," ensuring that the state does not overstep its constitutional boundaries.
